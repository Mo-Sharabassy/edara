# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

B2B buyers across several roles — Head of Marketing (planning launches and brand activations),
Head of Operations (buying outcomes and offloading headcount), startup founders (delegating
the entire events function), and corporate events managers (outsourcing overflow). The common
thread: they are time-poor professionals who know what a finished event looks like but don't
want to run the logistics themselves. They evaluate vendors quickly and trust signals —
portfolio, copy precision, visual coherence — more than features lists.

## Product Purpose

Edara is a professional events agency that operates as an outsourced events department:
exhibition booths, sponsorships, logistics, on-site production, end-to-end. The positioning is
"Events as a Service" — one brief in, a finished event out. The site must prove, by its own
quality, that Edara has the taste and operational precision to be trusted with a client's
brand in public.

## Brand Personality

Efficiency · One POC · Integration

Quiet operational confidence. Not luxury, not startup-flashy — the energy of a senior
department you didn't have to hire. The site should feel like handing something to someone
who has done it a hundred times.

## Anti-references

- AI-generated-looking layouts: generic structure, disconnected sections, no visual through-line
- Inconsistent desktop/mobile experience: what works at 1440px looks like a different site at 375px
- Decorative complexity with no substance behind it: heavy animation that doesn't communicate anything
- SaaS metric-grid heroes and "01 / 02 / 03" scaffolding (already removed)
- Corporate event company clutter: logo soup, low hierarchy, dated

Primary concern: visual synergy — the site must feel like one considered thing, not assembled
from parts. Each section should feel like it belongs to the same voice.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Edara's claim is expertise and precision. Every layout decision,
   spacing choice, and typographic call is a demonstration of that claim. Sloppiness anywhere
   is a credibility gap.

2. **Make complexity invisible.** The value proposition is that the client doesn't have to
   think about the details. Design should feel effortless — no visual noise, no competing
   hierarchies, no information that isn't earning its place.

3. **One voice, every viewport.** Mobile is not a degraded version of desktop. The same
   considered decisions apply at 320px and 1440px. Responsive is a design constraint, not
   an afterthought.

4. **Coherence over decoration.** Visual synergy — shared rhythm, consistent typographic
   voice, colour that connects sections rather than startles — matters more than any
   individual flourish.

5. **Utility earns trust.** The buyer is practical. Show outcomes, not process. Copy and
   layout should answer "what do I get?" before "how impressive are you?"

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. 4.5:1 minimum contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components.
Full keyboard navigation. Meaningful alt text on all imagery. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
for all animations (already partially implemented in the codebase).
